Services

⁠Dry Needling

Dry needling involves inserting thin, sterile needles into specific trigger points or tight muscles to release tension and reduce pain. This technique helps to improve muscle function and mobility by targeting areas of deep muscle tightness and promoting natural healing.

Cupping Therapy

Cupping therapy uses suction cups placed on the skin to create negative pressure, which increases blood flow and encourages healing in targeted areas. This ancient technique helps to relieve muscle tension, reduce inflammation, and promote relaxation.

Guasha Massage

Guasha massage is a traditional scraping technique that uses a smooth-edged tool to apply pressure to the skin, promoting circulation and reducing muscle tightness. It helps to break down scar tissue, release tension, and enhance the body’s natural healing processes.

Presso Therapy

Presso therapy, or compression therapy, uses air pressure to stimulate lymphatic drainage and improve circulation. This treatment is effective for reducing swelling, alleviating muscle fatigue, and accelerating recovery, particularly in the legs and lower body.

Manual Therapy

Manual therapy involves hands-on techniques such as joint mobilizations, soft tissue manipulation, and stretching to alleviate pain and improve mobility. This approach is highly effective for treating musculoskeletal issues by targeting specific areas of dysfunction.

Electrotherapy

Electrotherapy uses electrical stimulation to reduce pain, improve muscle strength, and enhance healing. It can help to increase circulation, reduce inflammation by stimulating the body’s natural healing mechanisms.

Who Am I?

My name is Edgard Lahoud, and I graduated from the Lebanese University, Faculty of Public Health, with a degree in Physical therapy. I am also a certified strength and conditioning coach. Since my teenage years, I pursued the dream of becoming a physical therapist, dedicating my university years to that goal while continuously envisioning a larger dream—opening my own clinic. In January 2024, I turned that dream into reality.
With two years of professional experience, I’ve worked with a wide range of cases, helping patients regain their confidence and live pain-free. My career has also taken me into the world of basketball, where I’ve worked with teams such as Champville D1 men, Antranik D1 men and women, D2 men, D3 Louaize, and many more athletes. Each experience has reinforced my dedication to helping people improve their movement, recover from injuries, and achieve their best physical potential.
